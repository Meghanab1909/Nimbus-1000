Topic: Variable Interest Rate

Definition:
A variable interest rate loan allows the interest rate to change over time based on market conditions or reference indexes.

Why it is risky:
Borrowers may start with a low interest rate, but the rate can increase significantly later, increasing monthly payments.

Common red-flag phrases:
- variable APR
- adjustable rate
- interest rate subject to change
- rate reset clause

Fair standard:
Loan agreements should clearly explain how and when interest rates may change.

Detection idea for AI:
Contracts mentioning adjustable interest rates should be analyzed for potential payment increases.
