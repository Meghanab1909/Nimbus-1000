Topic: General Loan Agreement Terms

Definition:
A loan agreement is a legal contract between a lender and a borrower that defines the conditions of borrowing money, including repayment schedule, interest rates, penalties, and obligations.

Why it matters:
Loan agreements often contain complex clauses that borrowers may not fully understand. Hidden fees, adjustable interest rates, and strict penalty conditions can significantly increase the cost of borrowing.

Common risky clauses:
- hidden administrative or processing fees
- variable interest rate adjustments
- early repayment penalties
- balloon payments
- aggressive default penalties

Fair standard:
Loan agreements should clearly disclose all fees, interest rates, repayment terms, and penalties in plain language so borrowers can make informed decisions.

Detection idea for AI:
The AI system should analyze the document to identify clauses related to fees, penalties, interest adjustments, or repayment structures that may negatively impact the borrower.
