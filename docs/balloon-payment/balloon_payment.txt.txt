Topic: Balloon Payment

Definition:
A balloon payment is a large lump sum that must be paid at the end of a loan term after smaller monthly payments.

Why it is risky:
Borrowers may believe the loan is affordable because monthly payments are low. However, the final balloon payment can be very large and difficult to pay.

Common red-flag phrases:
- balloon payment due at maturity
- final lump sum payment
- remaining balance due at end of term
- large final payment

Fair standard:
A fair loan should clearly disclose the existence and amount of any balloon payment so borrowers understand the full cost of the loan.

Detection idea for AI:
Contracts mentioning large final payments or balloon structures should be flagged as potentially risky clauses.
