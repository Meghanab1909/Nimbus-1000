Topic: Predatory Lending Signs

Definition:
Predatory lending refers to unfair or abusive loan practices that exploit borrowers through hidden fees, misleading terms, or extremely high interest rates.

Common warning signs:
- excessive interest rates
- hidden charges
- confusing contract language
- pressure to sign quickly
- penalties that trap borrowers in debt

Why it is risky:
These practices increase borrower debt and make repayment difficult.

Fair standard:
Loan terms should be transparent, clearly explained, and free from hidden conditions.

Detection idea for AI:
If loan documents contain hidden fees, confusing clauses, or aggressive penalties, they should be flagged as potential predatory terms.
